Skechers Email Audit

Thanks for completing your Skechers Plus account

2026-03-12 · SKECHERS PLUS <no-reply@emails.skechers.com>
Business Impact Score: 6/10

Executive Summary

Evidence

Discoverability suffers because every merch tile (six IGODigital recs plus two category grids and a “Shop Additional Styles” matrix) is just an unlabeled image with “PRODUCT” alt text—no names, prices, or benefit tie-in, so there’s no reason to click beyond curiosity (message.html product grid).

UX is overwhelmingly image-based: the “1000 BONUS POINTS” hero, benefits list, barcode, and even “Shop Women/Men/Kids” are flattened JPEGs with text baked in, so mobile readers and accessibility tools can’t parse the offer or CTAs (message.html hero + benefits art).

Email-to-site continuity is weak because there is no CTA that promises “View My Rewards” or “Apply $5 at checkout”; every link routes to generic commerce destinations (Shop Women, Shop Sale, app download, SMS opt-in) leaving the new member guessing how/where the reward lives.

Bugs/friction: the multipart email ships with an empty plain-text body (message.txt is blank), so text-only clients and deliverability checks see nothing; the only redemption guidance lives in a tiny 12px legal line saying “Points must be redeemed in your account,” which contradicts the lack of an account link.

Ease of use erodes further at the footer where an unrelated “Extra 10% Off In-Store” barcode and a wall of utility links repeat, forcing a very long scroll for what should be a quick confirmation moment.

Recommendations

  • Replace the image-only hero with live copy plus a real “Apply $5 Reward” button that deep-links into the signed-in Rewards wallet (or launches a lightweight webview explaining how to redeem in two steps). Surface expiry timing right there.
  • Turn the benefits block into HTML text with icons so it’s legible in dark mode and assistive tech, and append a short checklist (“1. Sign in, 2. Choose Reward at checkout, 3. Enjoy free shipping”) to remove guesswork.
  • Cut the duplicate nav slabs and SMS/App promos from this triggered message; instead, dedicate that space to two or three curated products that explicitly show “Costs $60 – only $55 after your reward” with names and prices to improve discoverability.
  • Ship a real text-only part plus descriptive alt text (e.g., “Skechers Plus 1,000-point reward banner”) so the email passes accessibility and spam heuristics.
  • If the in-store 10% barcode must stay, isolate it in a secondary module with clearer labeling (“Bonus perk: in-store shoppers only”) or move it into a separate retail trigger so it doesn’t distract from the primary digital task.
  • Natural next step: once updated creative is ready, QA the HTML/text pair and confirm the reward CTA actually deep-links into the account experience before re-sending this trigger.
