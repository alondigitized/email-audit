It's Back in Stock at SKECHERS.com

FromSKECHERS <no-reply@emails.skechers.com>
Received2026-03-13 17:20 UTC
Score5/10

Executive Summary

This is a back-in-stock behavioral trigger email — campaign ID TRG_US_EN_BROWSEINSTOCK_1_03202025 — targeting a customer who previously browsed a product that went out of stock. The trigger mechanic is the right call: high purchase intent, high relevance window. But the execution underdelivers on the promise. The subject line is fully anonymous ("It's Back in Stock at SKECHERS.com") when it should name the product. The hero image has no alt text. All image URLs use HTTP, not HTTPS. There is zero plain-text fallback. The email leans on visual assets to do the heavy lifting, and those assets are brittle. For a triggered message — where personalization is the entire point — the lack of it is the core failure.

The trigger strategy is sound and the preheader creates urgency, but execution gaps (anonymous subject, HTTP images, no alt text on hero, missing plain text) reduce deliverability confidence and accessibility reach. A triggered email this behavioral should score 8+. It doesn't because the personalization payoff — the specific product — never makes it into the subject or apparent above the fold.

What’s Working

  • Trigger mechanic is correct. A back-in-stock notification targets a customer at peak intent. This is exactly the right moment to send.
  • Preheader is serviceable. "The Skechers you loved are back and selling fast! Get yours now." — urgency language, paired with scarcity cue, is appropriate for the trigger type.
  • Standard template structure is consistent. Header nav (Account, Help), footer utility links (Customer Service, Privacy Policy, Terms of Use, Email Us) are all present and expected in Skechers CRM sends.
  • Krux/Salesforce DMP tracking is wired in correctly — audience matching pixels are firing, indicating proper downstream retargeting infrastructure.

What’s Weak

  • Subject line is anonymous. "It's Back in Stock at SKECHERS.com" does not name the product. A customer who browsed a specific style should see that style name or SKU in the subject. This is a triggered email — the data exists. Not using it is a missed conversion.
  • Hero image has no alt text. The primary image (041c30ac) carries no alt attribute. If images are blocked (common in corporate email clients), the email's main message is invisible.
  • All images served over HTTP. Three content images use http://image.emails.skechers.com — not HTTPS. Modern email clients and corporate proxies block mixed-content HTTP assets. This is a deliverability and rendering risk.
  • Plain-text fallback is completely absent. QA confirms 0 characters. This is both a CAN-SPAM best-practice failure and a signal to spam filters. Triggered emails should always have a text fallback.
  • No visible personalization in subject or preheader. Beyond the trigger itself, there's no first name, no product name, no color/size hint. The preheader "the Skechers you loved" is generic copy that could apply to any customer in any email.
  • Second logo image also missing alt text (f9b5ed32). If images fail, the email has no readable identity.

Recommendations

  • Add product name to the subject line. Format: "[Product Name] is back — don't miss it" or "Your [Style Name] is back in stock". The data pipeline already powers this trigger; the merge tag should be trivial.
  • Upgrade all image CDN URLs to HTTPS. image.emails.skechers.com must serve over TLS. Audit the entire asset library — this is likely systemic, not isolated to this send.
  • Write alt text for every content image. At minimum: the hero product image and the Skechers logo. Alt text is table-stakes accessibility and a failsafe for image-blocked rendering.
  • Generate a plain-text version. At minimum: subject, product name, a short urgency line, and the CTA URL. ESP tooling (Salesforce Marketing Cloud) can auto-generate this — verify it's enabled in the template config.
  • Add first-name personalization to preheader. "Hi [First Name] — the Skechers you loved are back" is a one-field change that increases open rate on triggered sends.
  • Add explicit in-stock quantity or urgency signal in body copy. "Only X left" or "Selling fast — limited sizes" reinforces the trigger premise and drives click urgency.

Bottom Line

The right email at the right moment, executed flat. Back-in-stock triggers only work when the customer immediately recognizes what they're being notified about. A generic subject destroys that recognition. Fix the subject, fix the images (HTTPS + alt text), generate a plain-text version, and this email goes from a 5 to an 8 without touching the offer or layout.

Evidence

Overall purpose of the email

Behavioral re-engagement trigger: notifying a customer that a previously browsed/viewed product is back in stock. Campaign TRG_US_EN_BROWSEINSTOCK_1_03202025. Legitimate and strategically correct send type.

Hero / primary value proposition

The hero module is image-dependent and the image carries no alt text. The preheader ("selling fast") is the only urgency signal visible before open. Post-open, the email relies on the hero image to communicate the product — but that image fails silently if blocked. No visible headline copy backs up the visual.

Membership / benefits section

Not present. No Skechers Elite mention, no loyalty points signal, no member-exclusive language. For a behavioral trigger this is acceptable — it's product-first, not membership-first — but a brief loyalty tie-in ("Your Elite points are waiting") would add secondary conversion incentive.

Product discoverability / recommendation modules

Based on what's visible, the email appears to be single-product focused (the back-in-stock item). No complementary product recommendations or "You might also like" carousel is evident. A triggered email of this type could reasonably include 2–3 related product tiles below the hero to expand basket opportunity.

Utility / secondary modules

Footer is standard and complete: Email Us, Account, Customer Service, Privacy Policy, Terms of Use. All links are routed through Skechers click-tracking (429 rate-limit responses during QA check — likely valid but throttled). Header offers Account and Help navigation. No social icons visible in the available markup.

Email-to-site continuity

Cannot fully assess without a rendered click-through, but the campaign structure (BROWSEINSTOCK) implies the CTA should land on the specific product page. If it lands on a generic back-in-stock landing page or homepage, that's a continuity failure worth flagging in a follow-up check.

Bugs / friction / clarity issues

HTTP images (3 of 3 content images): http://image.emails.skechers.com — non-HTTPS, blocked in many environments. This is a systemic infrastructure issue, not one-off.

Missing alt text on hero and secondary logo images: Silent failures when images are blocked. CAN-SPAM accessibility risk.

Zero-length plain-text body: QA flag [FAIL]. Not a minor warning — this is a hard gap in email hygiene for a production send.

Subject line carries no product identity: The defining feature of a back-in-stock trigger (the product) is absent from the most-read field in the inbox.

SPF/DKIM authentication status unknown via AgentMail relay — no Authentication-Results header surfaced. Worth confirming sender authentication is clean on the sending domain.
