Skechers Email Audit

Welcome to Skechers!

2026-03-11 21:18 · SKECHERS <no-reply@emails.skechers.com>
Business Impact Score: 5/10

Executive Summary

The welcome email still reads like a brochure of every Skechers touchpoint, so the 20% incentive never turns into a guided first purchase. The hero CTA, category tiles, and even the “Shop Additional Styles” nav all bounce to broad pages with no pre-filtered picks or auto-applied code, then the body devolves into SMS/app/pay-later promos before a shopper ever sees an actual product story. Net effect: discoverability stays low, continuity to the site is weak, and UX friction (manual code entry, buried expiration, broken legal URL) undercuts confidence.

Evidence & Analysis

The hero button and CTA stack all route to the same generic us-email-welcome-journey landing while forcing the shopper to copy/paste code WEL20-C39C3FPRRN, so there’s no curated first step or auto-applied offer (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:33, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:38, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:41).

Discoverability hinges on four generic search URLs—Street, Casual, Athletic, Work—none of which carry the welcome message, pricing, or best-seller context, so the user has to start shopping from scratch (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:44, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:47, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:52, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:55).

The next block repeats the entire primary nav (Women, Men, Kids, Clothing, New Arrivals, Sale), so a new subscriber gets 10+ undifferentiated CTAs before any guidance or proof that the coupon works (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:69, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:75, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:78, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:80, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:83, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:86, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:89).

Utility modules (SMS opt-in, app download, curbside, pay-later, store locator) dominate the fold immediately after this, further distracting from purchase discovery and creating UX fatigue before the subscriber even sees a product carousel (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:95, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:99, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:107, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:115, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:119, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:121, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:124, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:127).

Skechers Plus and account creation links surface only as generic links with no benefit framing, so the key loyalty/ease-of-use messages never reinforce why someone should create an account right now (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:60, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:134, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:137).

The legal footer still lists the offer expiry and redemption instructions only in fine print—plus the URL is malformed (http://www.skechers.com/www.Skechers.com), which is a credibility bug for anyone who tries to click it (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:157, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:158).

What Should Change

Build a true “Welcome” landing page (or parameterized PDP grid) that preselects best sellers by gender/occasion and auto-applies the welcome code so the first click feels guided; mirror that structure in the hero and replace the manual copy block (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:33, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:38).

Swap the four search links for merchandised stories (e.g., “Top Comfort Tech for Workwear” with 2-3 SKUs and Social proof) instead of dumping shoppers onto raw query pages (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:44, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:55).

Collapse the duplicated nav + service modules into a single “Next Steps” strip at the footer; keep the primary body focused on product discovery so users aren’t overwhelmed before they shop (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:69, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:95).

Move account creation and Skechers Plus messaging directly under the hero with a benefits checklist and links that flow into onboarding instead of isolated footer buttons (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:60, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:134).

Surface the expiration date and redemption instructions next to the offer (and fix the malformed legal URL) so there’s no trust gap at checkout (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:157, reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-welcome-to-skechers-fresh/message.txt:158).
