Skechers Email Audit

Thanks for completing your Skechers Plus account

2026-03-12 05:17 · SKECHERS PLUS <no-reply@emails.skechers.com>
Business Impact Score: 6/10

Executive Summary

Discoverability and UX take a hit because nearly every meaningful detail (benefits, CTAs, category links) is trapped in images and duplicated modules that force unnecessary scrolling.

Email-to-site continuity is shaky: “Redeem now” never sets the expectation of landing in the rewards wallet, and even the personalization grid shows unlabeled products, so shoppers click blind.

Evidence & Analysis

The only mentions of the $5 reward live in the hidden preheader and a full-bleed hero image, so there is no selectable text telling members how or where to redeem their 1,000 points (reports/email-artifacts/2026-03-12-thanks-for-completing-your-skechers-plus-account-fresh/message.html:195, reports/.../message.html:275).

The “Check out your benefits” block is another single image, so perks like free shipping or 90-day returns have zero explanatory copy, dates, or links to policy details (reports/.../message.html:277).

The recommendations grid headline even renders “WE THOUGHT YOU;D LIKE THESE…” with a punctuation error, and each tile’s alt text is just “PRODUCT,” leaving shoppers with no names, prices, or context before they click (reports/.../message.html:279, reports/.../message.html:289).

Category menus and promos pile up—women/men hero tiles, “Shop additional styles” menu, and two consecutive “Follow Us” sections—without additional insight, creating noise instead of guidance (reports/.../message.html:343, reports/.../message.html:360, reports/.../message.html:600).

Cross-channel promos (text program, app download, BNPL, curbside, store finder) show up mid-email but never connect back to Skechers Plus status or explain why a loyalty member should care about them now (reports/.../message.html:454, reports/.../message.html:495, reports/.../message.html:533).

The “Extra 10% off in-store” barcode appears as another graphic with no copyable code or clarification on whether it stacks with the $5 reward or how to surface it again inside the account or app (reports/.../message.html:347).

What Should Change

Under the hero, add live text that spells out “You now have 1,000 points ($5). Sign in → My Rewards → Tap Redeem” and include a real button that deep-links to the rewards wallet so the promised action is obvious (reports/.../message.html:275).

Rebuild the benefits module in HTML with short bullets, timelines (e.g., “Birthday reward drops 7 days before your date”), and links to policy details instead of a static image (reports/.../message.html:277).

Replace the igodigital image grid with cards that expose product name, price, and CTA labels so shoppers know what they’ll see after the click and fix the “YOU;D” typo in the header (reports/.../message.html:279, reports/.../message.html:289).

Collapse the redundant nav/social/app/BNPL promos into one short utility footer and repurpose the saved space for an “Explore ways to spend your $5” module featuring 2–3 curated categories instead of generic site-wide menus (reports/.../message.html:343, reports/.../message.html:360, reports/.../message.html:495).

Pair the in-store barcode with text instructions (“Show this barcode or use code XXXXX at checkout; cannot stack with other offers”) and surface the same code inside the account so members don’t lose it (reports/.../message.html:347).

Next Steps

Provide the rewards-wallet deep link and any copy guidelines so I can rewrite the hero/benefits modules accordingly.
