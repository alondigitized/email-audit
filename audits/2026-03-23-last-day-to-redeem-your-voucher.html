Fromadidas <adidas@us-news.adidas.com>

Score6/10

1. Executive Summary

A well-structured urgency email nudging adiClub members to redeem an expiring 15% voucher. The layout is clean and the hierarchy is sensible, but a critical character-encoding bug in the voucher section damages credibility at the most important moment, and a secondary module dilutes the urgency focus. The email does enough to drive redemption for engaged members, but leaves conversion on the table.

2. Business Impact Score: **6 / 10**

3. What's Working

  • Urgency framing is clear. "Last day to redeem" in the subject + product picks upfront creates a coherent moment of action.
  • Promo code is prominently displayed. The code WELCOME-1325-HMW is surfaced in a button-style element above the REDEEM ONLINE CTA — hard to miss.
  • Two redemption paths offered. Online checkout + in-store option (show email to staff) covers both use cases cleanly.
  • Product picks above the fold. Three product tiles with visible imagery give members something concrete to shop toward.
  • Clean visual hierarchy. The email reads top-to-bottom without confusion — voucher → profile CTA → app download.

4. What's Weak

  • Critical encoding bug in the voucher headline. The headline renders as HEREÂ€™S 15% OFF instead of HERE'S 15% OFF. This is visible in the email body and undermines trust at the highest-value moment in the email.
  • Profile completion module undercuts urgency. "Complete Your Profile for 50 Points" is a low-stakes retention ask that competes directly with the time-sensitive voucher message. It belongs in a different send.
  • Product tiles are small and under-merchandised. Three tiny thumbnail images with no clear product names or prices visible — they don't create enough desire to drive a click.
  • No explicit deadline reinforcement in the body. The subject says "last day" but the body only references "March 24, 2026" in fine print. A visible countdown or bolded expiry date in the hero would strengthen conversion.

5. Recommendations

1. Fix the encoding bug immediately. The HEREÂ€™S rendering is a production-level defect. Audit the voucher content block for character encoding issues before the send window closes.

2. Remove or defer the profile completion module. This is not the right send for it. Strip it out or move it below the app CTA to reduce cognitive load on the primary action.

3. Add expiry date to the hero. Make "Expires March 24" bold and prominent inside the voucher section, not buried in an asterisked footnote.

4. Upgrade the product tiles. Larger images, visible product names, and price callouts would make "top picks" feel like actual recommendations rather than decorative thumbnails.

6. Bottom Line

The email has sound bones — urgency-led, clear promo code, dual redemption paths — but the character encoding bug in the voucher headline is a credibility wound that needs to be fixed. If the encoding issue is resolved and the profile module removed, this becomes a solid 8/10 conversion email.

Hero / primary value proposition: "TOP PICKS FOR YOU" leads, followed by three product thumbnails and a plain-language reminder about the expiring voucher. Functional but the product images don't do enough selling.

Plain-text payload is also dominated by raw URLs, creating a deliverability risk.
