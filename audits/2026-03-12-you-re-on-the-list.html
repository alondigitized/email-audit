Skechers Email Audit

You're on the list!

Business Impact Score: 3/10

Executive Summary

Evidence & Findings

Discoverability: The only product mention is the raw string “Skechers Slip-ins: GO WALK Glide-Step Bliss 11.0,” with no imagery, copy, price, sizing, or adjacent options, so there’s nothing to guide shoppers toward the right variant or a fallback if their size/color is gone.

UX / Ease of Use: The body is just a stack of naked links (“web version,” PDP URL, duplicate customer-support links, repeated 1-800 number) with no CTA button or scannable hierarchy, which reads like a system log rather than a customer experience.

Email-to-site continuity: Subject promises “You’re on the list!” but the PDP link drops shoppers into a generic Slip-ins page without any explanation that their wait-listed size is now available or reserved; there’s no mention of timing, quantity, or exclusivity even though the UTM tag (TRG_US_EN_OUTOFSTOCK) signals an out-of-stock trigger.

Bugs / friction: Critical creative assets clearly failed (only the raw URLs and compliance footer survived), the preview line collapses to “Skechers(R),” and the footer repeats support info three times—strong evidence the HTML template or personalization logic is broken.

Ease-of-use gap: Without even a text CTA (“Shop now”), shoppers have to guess which bare URL leads to the product, which is especially risky on mobile where long URLs wrap awkwardly.

What Should Change

Fix the triggered template so both HTML and text variants include hero art, headline, price, key benefit bullets, and a clear CTA pointing to the specific Slip-ins PDP.

Use the waitlist data: call out the size/color that’s back, give a reservation window (“available for 24 hours”), and surface nearby alternatives or “notify me for other colors” to keep the shopper engaged if their variant is already gone.

Add a continuity module (e.g., “Here’s what to expect when you click”) so the PDP experience feels like a continuation of the email rather than a cold hand-off; include store pickup/in-stock flags if applicable.

Clean up the footer and customer-service block so each element appears once, and ensure the text-only fallback has readable copy instead of raw tracking links.
