# Walker Miles — Skechers.com Mobile Journey Review

Date: March 28, 2026 | Yesterday's Score: 4/10

Executive Summary

Well, I went through the whole thing again today on my phone and I'll be straight with you — it's still a bumpy ride. The site looks decent enough, found my Hands Free Slip-ins without too much trouble, but then hit a wall when it counted: the cart ended up completely empty after I tried to add a shoe. That's a deal-breaker. Feels like I went to the store, picked up a shoe, walked to the register, and they told me my basket was empty. The homepage still greeted me like I'm a woman shopping for wedges, even though I'm a logged-in Skechers Plus member. Some things got a tiny bit clearer in the navigation, but the fundamental problems are still there.

Business Impact Score: 4/10

Same score as yesterday. No meaningful improvement. Core conversion path remains broken.

What's Working

  • BOGO 50% OFF is front and center — that big promotional banner at the top and the sticky bar at the bottom registered clearly. Good visibility on the deal, I noticed it right away.
  • "Hands Free Slip-Ins" is a direct link in the Men's menu — once I tapped MENU and went to Men's, I could see "Hands Free Slip Ins" listed right there without digging. That's a win. Saved me from hunting.
  • Product page is readable — the Azusah product page had a big clear price ($135.00), decent-sized size grid buttons, and the ADD TO CART button was prominent. Text was large enough that I didn't have to squint.
  • 258 results on the Slip-ins page — solid selection. Good to see there are lots of options.
  • Free pickup callout on category page — "Free pickup at 364 locations" was a nice touch for someone like me who might want to try them on first.
  • BUY 1 GET 1 50% OFF shown on product page — reminded me the deal applies at cart, which I appreciated.

What's Weak

  • Cart is empty — the add-to-cart completely failed. This is the same critical bug as yesterday. I went through the whole journey, got to the cart, and it said "There are no items in your cart." That's a total conversion failure. This is the #1 problem on this site right now.
  • Homepage defaults to Women's — again. Even after logging in, the first thing I saw was women's shoes. I'm a 62-year-old man who's bought Skechers before. Why am I looking at the Hotshot Kickoff Women's shoe as the hero product? That's not me.
  • The email popup hits immediately. First thing I'm trying to do is look at the site and a "Unlock 20% OFF" modal slaps me in the face. I'm already a Skechers Plus member — why are you trying to get my email? Feels tone-deaf.
  • Login didn't appear to personalize anything. Step 3 showed the same homepage I saw before logging in. No "Welcome back, Walker" — nothing. The logged-in state made zero visible difference.
  • Add to cart required size selection but didn't communicate it well enough. Step 7 shows "Please select a size" — that's fine as validation goes, but the message was small and I had to hunt for what went wrong. Could have been clearer upfront.
  • Search for "slip in" returned 13,157 results — that's overwhelming. And the results were mixed men's and women's with no automatic filter for my gender even though I'm logged in as a man. A 62-year-old isn't going to page through 13,000 results.
  • 0 reviews on the product page. The star rating widget shows up but says "0 Reviews." That erodes my confidence. If nobody's reviewed it, how do I know if it's comfortable for older feet?

Recommendations

1. Fix the cart bug immediately. This is a revenue-killing defect. Every session that ends with an empty cart is a lost sale. Priority one, no debate.

2. Personalize the homepage for logged-in members. If I'm signed in and my purchase history is Men's Slip-ins, default my homepage to Men's — or at least remember I tapped Men's last time. A/B test a "Welcome back" hero for Plus members.

3. Suppress the email popup for logged-in Plus members. I'm already in the loyalty program. Asking me for my email is pointless and annoying. Show me my points balance or a member-exclusive deal instead.

4. Filter search results by gender for logged-in users. If I search "slip in" while logged in as a male, default the results to Men's. Let me opt into seeing women's if I want.

5. Make size selection more obvious before the button. Put a clear "Choose your size" prompt above the ADD TO CART button with better visual weight — not just a small error after I tap.

6. Surface reviews or social proof on the product page. If a product has no reviews, show "Be the first to review" or pull in ratings from similar products. An empty review widget makes the shoe look new and untested.

Bottom Line

I'm a loyal Skechers Plus member who came to this site ready to buy a $135 pair of Slip-ins and left with an empty cart and no purchase. The navigation to find the shoe was actually decent today — the Men's menu with Hands Free Slip-Ins as a direct link was helpful — but everything after that fell apart. The site doesn't know who I am even when I'm logged in, and the broken cart makes all of that irrelevant anyway.

Score: 4/10 — No change from yesterday. The browsing experience is tolerable but the purchase experience is broken.

## Technical Audit

## Technical Summary

Platform: Salesforce Commerce Cloud (SFCC), evidenced by /on/demandware.static/Sites-USSkechers-Site/ paths. Bot protection via Kasada (x-kpsdk-v). Consent management via TrustArc. Tag management via GTM (GTM-TDRBQZ). Heavy third-party payload: Monetate, Quantum Metric, Bloomreach, Stylitics, iGoDigital, plus 10+ ad/tracking pixels. 37 axe violations detected across the journey; 2 broken third-party integrations.

## Accessibility

Critical (must fix):

  • image-alt — <img> elements missing alt attributes on Homepage and Men's Shoes. Screen readers get no product context.
  • aria-required-parent (Homepage) / aria-required-children (View Product) — ARIA role hierarchy broken; assistive tech will misinterpret widget structure.
  • button-name (View Product) — Icon-only buttons with no accessible label. Likely size/color selectors or carousel controls.

Serious:

  • link-name (Homepage, Men's Shoes) — Anchor elements with no text or aria-label; probably image links or icon links.
  • link-in-text-block (Men's Shoes) — Links differentiated only by color in body text, fails WCAG 1.4.1.
  • aria-dialog-name / aria-input-field-name (View Product) — Modal dialogs and form inputs missing accessible names; likely size selector or popup overlays.

Moderate:

  • landmark-no-duplicate-main / landmark-main-is-top-level (Homepage) — Multiple <main> elements detected. Indicates injected content (Monetate, widgets) adding its own landmark structure, conflicting with page <main>.
  • landmark-unique / landmark-complementary-is-top-level — <aside> elements nested inside other landmarks instead of at document root.
  • region (Homepage, Men's Shoes) — Page content exists outside any landmark region; skip-navigation and screen reader region browsing incomplete.

Total reported: 37 violations across the journey; 17 additional not listed above.

## SEO

  • Placeholder GTM property ID: G-1234567890 appears in <script src="gtag/destination?id=G-1234567890"> across multiple pages alongside legitimate IDs (G-XZTV9LQ9DQ, AW-1066305778, DC-9630990). This is a test/default value that should not be in production; it may result in spurious GA4 hits.
  • Duplicate script tags: tags.creativecdn.com/dhGobFICKSkk6J9nvQPw.js appears twice in Men's Category HTML. s.yimg.com/wi/ytc.js appears twice on Homepage and Login. Duplicate tags inflate payload and can cause double-fired tracking events.
  • Inline <style> blocks injected at <head> top by TrustArc before any page CSS — not a crawl blocker but delays render start.
  • LCP and CLS metrics were not captured; without these, Core Web Vitals CrUX status is unknown and Google Search ranking signal assessment is incomplete.

## Mobile Optimization

  • --vh custom property (--vh: 11.89px) set via JS — correct pattern to work around iOS Safari's dynamic viewport height issue with 100vh. Implemented correctly.
  • Web Font Loader (wf-mulish-n3 through n7-active) — loading 5 Mulish weight variants. Blocking FOUT is handled (classes applied after load), but 5 font weights is aggressive for mobile bandwidth. Assess which weights are actually used.
  • vice-managed-src on Pinterest, Facebook, Yahoo, Impact, Taboola, Nextdoor scripts — TrustArc consent management gates these. Correct implementation: scripts won't fire pre-consent. However, if consent banner is dismissed, all these fire simultaneously, causing a main-thread spike.
  • No viewport meta tag visible in truncated source — not necessarily absent, but should be confirmed: <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1"> is required.

## Console & Network Errors

1. 429 — Kasada bot protection endpoint:

`

https://www.skechers.com/149e9513-.../fp?x-kpsdk-v=j-1.2.291

`

Kasada's fingerprint challenge returning rate-limit. In an audit/automated browser context this is expected (bot detection triggering). In production for real users this would indicate Kasada flagging the session, potentially blocking subsequent requests. No action needed for audit context, but warrants monitoring in prod RUM.

2. CORS block — Stylitics stylesheet:

`

https://web-assets.stylitics.com/style.css?base=hotspots&version=1f1e003a2c

`

Blocked by CORS policy (No 'Access-Control-Allow-Origin'). The Stylitics outfit/hotspot widget will render without its stylesheet. This is a Stylitics-side misconfiguration — their CDN is not returning the correct CORS headers for skechers.com as an allowed origin. Requires Stylitics support ticket or SDK version update (1f1e003a2c may be an expired build).

3. 422 — iGoDigital cart tracking:

`

https://nova.collect.igodigital.com/c2/100007688/track_cart?payload={"url":"...","user_info":{"details":{}}}

`

Salesforce Marketing Cloud personalization endpoint rejecting the cart tracking call. The user_info.details object is empty {} — likely missing required identity fields (email, subscriber key) because the session is unauthenticated or the identity resolution failed. This breaks cart abandonment triggers and MC personalization data. Fix: ensure user_info population is gated on authenticated state or include anonymous visitor ID.

9. Instrument LCP and CLS collection — current audit has no CLS data for any step and no LCP data at all. Add RUM or extend audit script to capture these before any Core Web Vitals regression analysis is possible.

10. Audit font weight loading — evaluate whether all 5 Mulish weights (n3–n7) are rendered on mobile; unused weights are wasted bytes on constrained connections.
