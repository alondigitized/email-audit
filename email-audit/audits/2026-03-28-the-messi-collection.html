Skechers Experience Review

The Messi collection

Fromadidas <adidas@us-news.comms.adidas.com>
Received2026-03-28 03:09 UTC
Score6/10

Business Impact Score: **6 / 10**

Strong creative foundation undercut by audience confusion and a misaligned secondary module.

What's Working

  • Hero image lands. The moody blue-tinted photo of Messi with a ball is cinematic and premium. "Built for Greatness" is a confident, on-brand headline that doesn't over-explain.
  • Samba Messi product shot is excellent. The teal/coral colorway pops against a clean background. This is the section the subject line promises and it delivers.
  • Top promotional banner is well-executed. The 30%-off offer with a clear "SHOP NOW" CTA is positioned correctly — visible without dominating the brand story.
  • Layout is clean and mobile-readable. Good use of whitespace, no cluttered grid, imagery dominates appropriately.

What's Weak

  • The Jude Bellingham module has no business being here. The subject line is "The Messi collection." A giveaway for "Win the Jude Newspaper" — featuring an entirely different athlete — is a jarring subject-to-content mismatch. Subscribers who open expecting Messi content hit a Bellingham contest. This is a retention risk.
  • "Built for Greatness" is vague as a hero headline. It works atmospherically but doesn't anchor a product or a collection launch. There's no immediate answer to "what am I being shown and why now?"
  • No product grid or "shop the collection" module. A collection launch email should surface multiple SKUs or at least link to a curated category. There's one product (the Samba Messi) and then the email moves on. Low commerce density for a collection announcement.
  • The Jude module copy is small and unclear. "Grab the offer for a chance to win a digital newspaper" reads weakly. The value proposition of the contest is not immediately legible at scroll speed.

Recommendations

1. Remove the Jude Bellingham module entirely from this send or create a separate send for it. A Messi collection email is not the place for a different-athlete contest — this belongs in a general newsletter or a Jude-specific campaign.

2. Add a product grid below the Samba Messi section. Show 3–4 Messi collection items (apparel, accessories, other footwear) with price + CTA. This is a collection launch — prove there's a collection.

3. Sharpen the hero headline. "The Messi Collection — Now Available" or "Samba Messi: Built for Greatness" ties the imagery to the product and the subject line directly.

4. Give the promotional banner more specificity if the 30% offer applies to Messi collection items — say so. If it doesn't, the banner creates a false expectation before the subscriber even scrolls.

Bottom Line

This email has the creative assets to be a high-performing collection launch. The Messi imagery is compelling, the product looks great, and the promotional mechanic is in place. What kills it is the Jude Bellingham module, which signals to the subscriber that adidas isn't sure what this email is about either. Strip that module, add a product grid, and this becomes a solid 8.

1. Technical Summary

Two critical failures: a broken click-redirect containing an invalid relative URL, and a completely absent plain-text fallback. Several images are served over HTTP and lack alt attributes, with duplicate @font-face blocks and a malformed charset <meta> adding minor rendering risk.

2. Link & Tracking Issues

[FAIL] Broken destination URL in click-redirect

The QA resolver found unknown url type: '/g/1c0e453b-372a-44c6-a47a-a76077af9933' embedded inside the following tracking wrapper:

`

https://dv.adidas.com/o/1c0e453b-372a-44c6-a47a-a76077af9933?cp_tp=v3.eJzjYu...

`

The inner destination path (/g/...) is a bare relative path, not an absolute URL. This means the redirect has no valid destination to forward to — the link is broken for any recipient who clicks it. This must be fixed before send.

[WARN] 31 tracking links not validated

All click-redirect destinations (via dv.adidas.com and click.comms.adidas.com) were skipped by the HTTP probe. Destination URL health for those 31 links is unconfirmed.

3. Rendering & Accessibility

[WARN] 7 images served over HTTP (mixed content)

The arrow image and one tracking pixel are served via plain HTTP, not HTTPS:

  • http://image.link.adidas.com/lib/fe6515707c62007e7715/m/3/b0c011a2-d468-414b-a8a2-8595b3ec7b34.jpg — appears 6 times
  • http://click.comms.adidas.com/CI0/0102019d326a9c73-47e3b8b6-d528-4ef2-828e-6812a6bc9c8d-000000/...

Modern email clients (Gmail, Outlook.com, Apple Mail) proxy or block HTTP image sources. These will likely render as broken images in a significant portion of clients.

[WARN] 2 images missing alt text

  • https://dv.adidas.com/o/1c0e453b-372a-44c6-a47a-a76077af9933?cp_tp=v3.eJzjYu... (no alt attribute)
  • http://click.comms.adidas.com/CI0/0102019d326a9c73... (tracking pixel — should have alt="" explicitly)

Missing alt text degrades screen reader accessibility and causes visible broken-image labels when images are blocked.

[WARN] Malformed charset <meta> tag

`html

<meta content="text/html; charset=UTF-8" />

`

Missing http-equiv="Content-Type". Correct form:

`html

<meta http-equiv="Content-Type" content="text/html; charset=UTF-8" />

`

Without http-equiv, some older Outlook and Lotus Notes clients may misinterpret encoding.

[INFO] Duplicate @font-face declarations

AdihausDIN and AdineuePRO are declared twice in two separate <style> blocks with identical src values. No functional breakage, but adds ~1 KB of unnecessary CSS payload and increases parse time.

[INFO] font-display: swap in email CSS

font-display: swap is a web font loading hint with no effect in any email client. Dead code — not a breakage risk.

No unresolved merge tokens visible in the truncated HTML source (no {{, %%, *|, or [% patterns). Cannot fully confirm without the complete source, but no failures detected in the provided excerpt.

QA reports text version is 0 characters. CAN-SPAM and CASL require a readable plain-text alternative. Several major ISPs (Yahoo, AOL) also use plain-text presence as a spam signal. This needs a populated text/plain MIME part.

[WARN] SPF/DKIM authentication status unconfirmed

Authentication-Results header was not found via the AgentMail relay. SPF/DKIM pass status for us-news.comms.adidas.com cannot be verified from available data. Recommend confirming alignment independently.

[INFO] Unsubscribe mechanism not assessed

The footer HTML was truncated. Cannot confirm presence of a functional one-click unsubscribe link or List-Unsubscribe header — these should be verified in the full send.

6. Email-to-Site Continuity

[UNVERIFIABLE] 31 click destinations not probed

Because all links are wrapped in dv.adidas.com and click.comms.adidas.com redirects, UTM parameter presence on landing pages could not be confirmed. The single resolvable link (the broken /g/ URL) has no valid destination, so landing page alignment cannot be checked for that CTA.

No naked (unwrapped) destination URLs were present in the available source to inspect for UTM parameters directly.
