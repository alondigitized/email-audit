## Executive Summary

A browse-abandonment recovery email for a single Skechers Slip-ins product. The concept is sound — retarget an interested shopper with the exact product they viewed plus complementary recommendations — but execution is mediocre. The hero product recommendation is present but small and low-impact, and a BOGO 50% Off promotional banner arrives late and feels disconnected from the recovery narrative. The email works as a functional reminder but misses its potential as a persuasive closer.

## Business Impact Score: 5 / 10

## What's Working

  • Clear browse-abandonment hook. "Still thinking about it? You've got great taste!" is warm and relevant — it confirms the email is personalized, which builds trust.
  • Named product in the hero. The Slip-ins Combine Foam — Cozy Fit is called out by name with a visible product image and a clear CTA ("Take Another Look"), reducing friction back to the PDP.
  • BOGO 50% Off banner. The offer adds a meaningful financial incentive that could tip a hesitant buyer. It's the strongest conversion lever in the email.
  • Utility footer. Texting opt-in, app download, curbside pickup, afterpay/Klarna, and store finder are all present — useful for shoppers at different stages of the purchase journey.

## What's Weak

  • Hero product image is tiny. The product the entire email is built around is rendered at a small size that doesn't showcase the shoe. For a browse-abandonment email, the product should be the visual centerpiece.
  • "More options we think you'll love" dilutes the message. The four recommendation tiles arrive immediately after the hero, shifting focus away from recovering the original browsed product before the case is even made. This competes with the primary CTA.
  • BOGO offer is buried and contextually orphaned. It appears after the recommendation row with no connective copy — there's no "…and here's a reason to buy now." It reads like a separate broadcast email grafted on.
  • Navigation links (Women, Men, Kids, Clothing, New Arrivals, Sale) are redundant here. A recovery email should reduce choice, not expand it. These links invite exit behavior.
  • No urgency or scarcity signal on the hero product. No "limited sizes," "selling fast," or offer expiry. The email gives the shopper no reason to act now vs. later.
  • Small imagery throughout. All product tiles in the recommendation row are very small. The visual hierarchy doesn't match the conversion intent of the email.

## Recommendations

1. Make the hero product much larger. Dedicate the top third of the email to the Combine Foam shoe — a lifestyle or close-up shot at full width would anchor the email's purpose.

2. Move the BOGO offer up, immediately below the hero CTA. Frame it as the reason to come back today: "And right now — Buy One, Get One 50% Off." This turns a fragmented offer into a compelling closing argument.

3. Cut or move the category nav links. They belong in broadcast emails, not recovery flows. Replace them with a single secondary CTA (e.g., "Shop All Slip-ins") at most.

4. Add urgency to the hero. Even a soft signal ("Back-in-stock risk" or "Offer ends soon") would improve conversion rate without being manipulative.

5. Trim the "More Options" row to 2 products max, and ensure they're clearly complementary to the browsed item — not generic bestsellers.

## Bottom Line

This email correctly identifies its job (recover a browsing session) but doesn't execute with enough conviction. The BOGO offer is the best tool available and it's hidden. The product is the emotional anchor and it's undersized. Reorganizing the hierarchy — big product, tight copy, prominent offer, single CTA — would materially improve click-through without any new creative investment.

## Technical Audit — SKECHERS "This is perfect for you!" (Browse Abandonment)

From: hello@msgs.skechers.com | ESP: Attentive (skechers.attentivemail.com)

1. Technical Summary

Standard table-based email built in Attentive, with mobile-responsive media queries. All links route through Attentive's click-tracking redirect (/ls/click?upn=); destination URLs and UTM parameters cannot be directly inspected from the source. Several issues flagged below around HTTP link schemes, an empty <title>, and a global CSS rule that suppresses link underlines.

2. Link & Tracking Issues

HTTP instead of HTTPS on all tracked links

Every href in the visible source uses http:// — both the click-tracking domain and the image CDN:

  • http://skechers.attentivemail.com/ls/click?upn=... (all CTAs and web version link)
  • http://image.emails.skechers.com/lib/fe3115707564047a731c78/... (logo/product images)

HTTP tracking links trigger mixed-content warnings in some security-conscious email clients and suppress browser HTTPS indicators when the subscriber clicks through. Attentive supports https:// redirects; this appears to be a configuration gap, not a platform limitation.

Destination URL UTM coverage unverifiable

Because all links pass through Attentive's opaque upn= redirect, the final landing page URLs cannot be inspected in the source. Needs spot-check via link expansion or Attentive's campaign link report to confirm utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign are appended.

3. Rendering & Accessibility

Empty <title> tag

`html

<title></title>

`

Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS in browser-view mode) announce the tab/document title; an empty value provides no context. Should be set to match the subject line or email description.

Global link decoration suppression

`css

#MessageViewBody a { color: inherit; text-decoration: none; }

`

This rule removes underlines from *all* links in Gmail's MessageViewBody context, including the unsubscribe link in the footer (if present). This can reduce unsubscribe visibility, which is both an accessibility concern (WCAG 1.4.1 — use of color alone) and a CAN-SPAM risk if the unsubscribe link relies solely on underline as its visual affordance. The unsubscribe link should have an explicit style="text-decoration:underline" override.

Preheader padding technique

The preheader uses both zero-width non-joiner characters (͏ U+034F) and soft hyphens (­ U+00AD) for inbox-preview padding — two different methods mixed in the same element. While functional, the soft hyphen (­) can render as a visible hyphen in some older clients (BlackBerry, some Android stock clients). Recommend using a single consistent method (zero-width joiners only).

Mobile breakpoint

@media (max-width:620px) is present with column stacking rules. No issues detected in the CSS logic visible.

4. Personalization & Merge Tokens

No unresolved/broken merge tokens are visible in the truncated source. The browse-abandonment nature of the email implies dynamic product blocks (images, names, prices, URLs) should be present in the body — these cannot be validated from the truncated HTML. Needs full-source review to confirm:

  • Product image src attributes contain populated values (not empty or placeholder tokens)
  • Product name/price text blocks contain no raw {{token}} fallbacks

Subject line ("This is perfect for you!") and preheader ("Take another look before these items sell out.") contain no personalization tokens — acceptable for this email type but worth noting if A/B testing first-name personalization.

5. Compliance

CAN-SPAM / unsubscribe — Cannot confirm from truncated source. The footer (not included) must contain:

  • A functioning, one-click unsubscribe mechanism
  • The sender's physical mailing address

The #MessageViewBody a { text-decoration:none } rule noted above warrants a manual check that the unsubscribe link has sufficient visual differentiation.

Authentication headers — Not assessable from HTML source alone. Should be verified at send time via MX Toolbox or email header inspection:

  • SPF: msgs.skechers.com sending domain should be authorized
  • DKIM: Signed by Attentive on behalf of skechers.com or msgs.skechers.com
  • DMARC: skechers.com policy should be p=quarantine or p=reject and aligned

6. Email-to-Site Continuity

UTM parameters on destination URLs cannot be confirmed due to Attentive redirect wrapping (see §2). Expected parameters for a browse-abandonment send:

  • utm_source=attentive (or email)
  • utm_medium=email
  • utm_campaign=browse_abandonment (or equivalent)
  • utm_content differentiating individual product CTAs

If the destination is a personalized cart/browse-recovery landing page, confirm the Attentive link-builder is appending these — they are not visible in the upn= encoded strings.
