Meet the Supernova Rise 3 Adaptive

Fromadidas <adidas@us-news.comms.adidas.com>
Received2026-03-27 02:04 UTC
Score5/10

1. Executive Summary

A well-intentioned product launch email for an adaptive athletic shoe, but the execution undersells the product. The hero image is small, the copy is generic, and the email quickly pivots to an unrelated product (Handball Spezial) and a geo-targeted module ("Gear Near You") that fragments focus. The result feels like a multi-product newsletter rather than a dedicated adaptive product launch — a missed opportunity to make a meaningful statement.

2. Business Impact Score: **5 / 10**

3. What's Working

  • Clear product name and positioning. "Supernova Rise 3 Adaptive" is called out plainly, and the one-line description ("made with athletes with disabilities") establishes purpose quickly.
  • Promo banner present. The "Save 30% on eligible styles with code MARCH" banner at the top captures discount-seekers immediately — good for click conversion.
  • Gear Near You module. Leopard-print imagery is visually arresting and the local store angle is a solid retention/foot-traffic tactic.
  • Clean layout. The email is legible, well-spaced, and not cluttered.

4. What's Weak

  • Hero image is too small and passive. The adaptive shoe is shown in a low-key, muted shot. For an adaptive athlete product, this needed a more powerful, human-centered visual — someone wearing the shoe in action. The image reads more like a product thumbnail than a hero.
  • Copy is thin. The body description is two short sentences. For a product positioned around disability inclusion, this feels like it needed more substance — a brief athlete story, a key feature callout, or a clear differentiator.
  • Focus is split. Handball Spezial appears as a second featured product with its own headline and body copy. It competes for the same visual hierarchy as Supernova Rise 3 Adaptive. This dilutes the adaptive product launch narrative entirely.
  • CTA is not visible on the hero section. There is no clearly visible button or CTA link on the Supernova Rise 3 Adaptive section — the primary product has no call to action in the render.
  • Gear Near You module is unexplained. The leopard-print shoe grid is visually dominant but the connection to the email's stated theme (adaptive product) is nonexistent. It feels algorithmically inserted.
  • Promo code is not tied to the hero product. The "MARCH" discount banner is floating at top — it's unclear whether it applies to the Adaptive shoe, which undermines urgency.

5. Recommendations

1. Lead with a human. Replace the product-only hero with an athlete-in-motion shot. Adaptive footwear earns brand equity through storytelling, not still-life photography.

2. Add a CTA button to the Supernova section. "Shop Supernova Rise 3 Adaptive" should appear directly below the product copy with a high-contrast button.

3. Remove Handball Spezial or relegate it to a secondary row. If this is a product launch email, protect the single-product focus. Handball Spezial belongs in a digest, not here.

4. Tie the promo to the featured product. Restate the code adjacent to the CTA: "Use MARCH for 30% off, including the Rise 3 Adaptive." This closes the conversion loop.

5. Expand body copy to 3–4 lines. Add one specific feature (e.g., adaptive closure system, wider fit, orthotic-compatible) to give the reader a reason to click beyond brand loyalty.

6. Bottom Line

The email has the right ingredients — adaptive product, promo code, local store engagement — but fails to unify them into a coherent narrative. The adaptive product launch is buried by a competing product and a geo module. With a stronger hero, a visible CTA, and tighter focus, this could be a brand-building and commercially effective send. As rendered, it's a middling multi-product email that doesn't do justice to either product.
